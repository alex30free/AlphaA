Methodology — 5-Step Funnel

01

Universe

275 Swedish stocks (Large, Mid & Small Cap) from Nasdaq Stockholm. Live prices fetched weekly via Yahoo Finance. F-Score data from Börsdata CSV — Piotroski's 9-signal financial health rating.

02

Outlier Removal

Remove top 5% by 1-month return — lottery stocks whose recent spikes are unlikely to persist. Also remove bottom 10% by market beta — stocks with near-zero market trend correlation.

03

12_2 Momentum

Rank all remaining stocks by 12-month return, skipping the most recent month (t-12 to t-1). Skip-month convention avoids short-term reversal contamination. Keep the top 50% by this score.

04

FIP Quality Filter

FIP = sign(12m) × (%negative days − %positive days). Lower FIP = more positive trading days = smoother trend. From the momentum top 50%, the 50 with the lowest FIP are kept. Eliminates "big jump" stocks.

05

Quality Gate (F-Score)

Piotroski F-Score ≥ 5 (out of 9) is a soft quality flag. Scores 7–9 = strong financial health. Shown in the table as a final sanity check. Does not hard-exclude but heavily informs conviction.

06

Final Top 20

From the FIP-filtered set, re-rank by 12_2 momentum. Top 20 published every Friday after Nasdaq Stockholm closes (~18:30 CET) via automated GitHub Actions.

What the Research Shows

Alpha Architect — "The Quantitative Momentum Investing Philosophy" (Vogel & Gray, 2016) · Jegadeesh & Titman (1993) · Asness, Moskowitz & Pedersen (2013)

Why Momentum Works

Stocks that outperformed over the past 12 months tend to continue outperforming over the next 3–12 months. Jegadeesh & Titman (1993) documented excess returns of ~1% per month for high-momentum stocks, replicated globally across decades.

The Skip-Month Rule

Using 12-month return excluding the most recent month (12_2) avoids short-term reversal — where the very best recent performers mean-revert. This is standard convention in all academic momentum research.

Frog-in-the-Pan

Stocks with gradual, consistent daily increases outperform stocks that gained the same return in a few big days. The FIP filter separates durable trend from volatile spike momentum — the key insight of the Alpha Architect approach.

Quality + Momentum

Combining momentum with Piotroski F-Score reduces "dead cat bounce" exposure. Stocks with strong momentum and improving fundamentals show more persistent outperformance with lower tail risk than pure price momentum alone.
